CENTRAL NERVOUS SYSTEM 

Central Nervous System (CNS) Tumors includes a large number of tumors of the brain and spine, ranging from benign and often slow growing meningiomas to high grade glioblastoma multiforme. Included in this group are tumors arising elsewhere in the body (for example breast and lung cancers) with metastases to the brain and spine.

Brain and spine tumors are often treated with combinations of surgery, chemotherapy and radiation therapy (RT). It is thus important for CNS tumor patients to be seen and treated at an institution such as UC San Diego with an experienced Multi-Disciplinary team comprised of neurosurgeons, neuro-oncologists, radiation oncologists, neuro-radiologists and neuro-phycologists, all specialists in the care of these patients.

We are committed to providing our CNS Tumor Patients access to all state-of-the-art treatment technologies including IMRT, IGRT, SRS and Proton Therapy.

Our CNS Tumor Specialists are an integral part of the UC San Diego Moores Cancer Center

We work closely with the Moores Cancer Center CNS Tumor Multi-disciplinary Disease Team and also with Medical Oncologists and Surgeons at Kaiser Permanente San Diego who take care of patients with brain and spine tumors. Department Brain Tumor Researchers have developed novel approaches to planning and delivering radiation therapy in these patients in an effort to minimize potential adverse effects on neurocognitive function. This work is the subject of a recent National Institutes of Health (NIH) R01 Grant.
