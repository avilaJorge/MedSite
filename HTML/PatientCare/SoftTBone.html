Soft Tissue and Bone Tumors

Multiple benign and multiple tumors may arise in soft tissue and bone, including malignant tumors of fatty tissue (liposarcoma), smooth muscle (leiomyosarcoma), or cartilage (chondrosarcoma). Soft tissue and bone tumors are also common in tumor including rhabdomyosarcoma (see Pediatric Cancers).

The treatment of soft tissue and bone tumors is complex and involves multi-specialists including orthopedic oncologists, medical oncologists and radiation oncologists. It is important for patients to be seen and treated at centers with an experienced multi-disciplinary team of physicians focusing on these patients.

We are committed to providing our Soft Tissue patients with the latest treatment approaches including IMRT, IGRT, Brachytherapy and Proton Therapy.

Our Soft Tissue and Bone Cancer Radiation Oncology specialists are members of the Moores Cancer Center Multi-disciplinary Soft Tissue/Bone Tumor Treatment Team and work closely with Kaiser Permanente San Diego Medical Oncologists and Surgeons who treat Soft Tissue and Bone malignancies.
