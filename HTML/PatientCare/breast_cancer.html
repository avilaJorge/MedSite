BREAST CANCER

Breast Cancer ranges from pre-invasive Ductal Carcinoma In-Situ (DCIS) to locally advanced/metastatic disease. Most patients have invasive ductal carcinoma; however, some patients havedifferent tumor histologies including lobular carcinoma or Phyllodes tumors.

Breast Cancer patients are treated with combinations or surgery, chemotherapy and/or radiation therapy (RT) depending on various clinicopathologic factors, including tumor size, grade, and lymph node involvement. It is important for all patients to be seen and treated by an experienced multi-disciplinary care team comprised of surgical oncologists, plastic surgeons, medical oncologists, radiation oncologists and radiologists who all specialize in the care of Breast Cancer patients.

We are committed to providing our Breast Cancer patients with the latest treatment approaches including IMRT, IGRT, Brachytherapy, and Proton Therapy.

Our Breast Cancer Radiation Oncology specialists are an integral part of the UC San Diego Moores Cancer Center

We work closely with the Moores Cancer Center Moores Cancer Center Multi-disciplinary Breast Cancer Treatment Team and also with Medical Oncologists and Surgeons at Kaiser Permanente San Diego who who treat Breast malignancies. UC San Diego Radiation Oncologists are national leaders in the use of accelerated partial breast irradiation (APBI) using brachytherapy. APBI Brachytherapy is an attractive alternative to whole breast irradiation in select patients, particularly those with small (< 3cm) completely excised tumors which are Estrogen Receptor positive (ER+).
