BREAST CANCER

Breast Cancer ranges from pre-invasive Ductal Carcinoma In-Situ (DCIS) to locally advanced/metastatic disease. Most patients have invasive ductal carcinoma; however, some patients have lobular carcinoma or Phyllodes tumors.

Breast Cancer patients are often treated with combinations or surgery, chemotherapy and/or radiation therapy (RT) depending on various clinicopathologic factors, including tumor size, grade, and lymph node involvement. It is important for all patients to be seen and treated by an experienced multi-disciplinary team comprised of surgical oncologists, plastic surgeons, medical oncologists, radiation oncologists and radiologists who all specialize in the care of Breast Cancer patients.

Central Nervous System (CNS) Tumor Types

Low Grade Glioma, Anaplastic Astrocytoma, Glioblastoma Multiforme, Ependymoma, CNS Lymphoma, Meningioma, Schwannoma, Pituitary Adenoma, Arteriovenous Malformation (AVM), Trigeminal Neuralgia, Brain Metastases, Chordoma & Chondrosarcoma, Spine Tumors

We are committed to providing our CNS Tumor Patients access to all state-of-the-art treatment technologies including IMRT, IGRT, SRS and Proton Therapy.

Our CNS Tumor Specialists are an integral part of the Moores Cancer Center CNS Tumor Multi-disciplinary Disease Team and also work closely with Medical Oncologists and Surgeons at Kaiser Permanente San Diego who take care of patients with brain and spine tumors.

Department Brain Tumor Researchers have developed novel approaches to planning and delivering RT in these patients in an effort to minimize potential adverse effects on neurocognitive function. This work is the subject of a recent National Institutes of Health (NIH) R-01 Grant. Click here to learn more about novel Department Brain Tumor Research.
