BENIGN TUMORS AND CONDITIONS

Multiple benign tumors and conditions are treated with radiation therapy, ranging from Dupuytren’s Contracture of the hands to Heterotopic Ossification, abnormal bone growth following trauma or surgery. An emerging area of interest is the use of radiation to treat abnormal cardiac rhythms such as ventricular tachycardia (VT).

Not included on the list at right are other benign tumors included under specific disease sites. Examples include meningioma and pituitary adenoma (see CNS Tumors), desmoid tumors (Soft Tissue and Bone Tumors).

We are proud to offer patients with all types of benign tumors and conditions access to Radiation Therapy which is an effective approach in many of these patients.

UCSD is developing a new center focused on non-invasive targeting and treatment focused on patients with cardiac arrhythmias. This cutting-edge treatment which uses a single fraction of radiation delivered using stereotactic body radiotherapy (SBRT). It is an ideal approach in patients who have not responded to standard treatment approaches.
