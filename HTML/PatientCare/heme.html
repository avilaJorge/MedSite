HEMATOLOGIC CANCERS

Hematologic Malignancies include a wide range of cancers including Hodgkin Lymphoma, Diffuse and Follicular Lymphommas, Multiple Myeloma and Acute and Chronic Leukemias. The treatment of many of these tumors involves a combination of chemotherapy and radiation therapy (RT). RT approaches range from very focal treatments in early stage Hodgkin Lymphoma to Total Body Irradiation (TBI) in patients with leukemia.

It is important for patients with hematologic malignancies to be seen and treated at a center with an experienced multi-disciplinary team of hematologists and radiation oncologists who focus on the treatment of these tumors. UC San Diego is a leader in the treatment of hematologic malignancies and the use of novel stem cell transplant and CAR T-Cell therapies.
