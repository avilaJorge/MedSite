Pediatric Cancers

A wide range of benign and malignant tumors treated with radiation therapy (RT) may occur in children, most commonly brain tumors. However, other commonly treated tumors include Wilm’s Tumor which arises from the kidneys and Rhabdomyosarcoma which is a tumor of muscle.

The treatment of pediatric cancers may involves multiple specialists including pediatric surgeons, pediatric medical oncologists and hematologists, and pediatric radiation oncologists. This team is supported by other specialists including radiologists and pathologists. Given the complexity of treatment, it is imperative that children with these tumors be seen and treated at a dedicated pediatric oncology center by an experienced multi-disciplinary treatment team.

We are committed to providing our Pediatric Cancer patients with the latest treatment approaches including IMRT, IGRT, SRS, SBRT, Brachytherapy and Proton Therapy.

Our Pediatric Cancer Radiation Oncology specialists are members of the Rady Children’s Hospital Multi-disciplinary Pediatric Cancer Treatment Team and work closely with Kaiser Permanente San Diego Medical Oncologists and Surgeons who treat Pediatric malignancies.

UCSD Radiation Oncologists work closely with Rady Children’s Hospital pediatricians at the California Proton Center ensuring all pediatric patients have access to state-of-the-art treatment. Our team also works with Children’s Hospitals throughout the California and beyond.
