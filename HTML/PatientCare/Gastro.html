GASTROINTENSTINAL CANCERS

Gastrointestinal (GI) Cancers include tumors of all parts of the GI tract, from the esophagus to the rectum and anus. Treatment typically entails a combination of surgery, chemotherapy and radiation therapy (RT), based on the characteristics of the tumor (site, histology, grade and stage). RT occupies an important role in nearly all GI tumors, typically delivered together with surgery and chemotherapy.

The treatment of GI tumors is challenging and patients are best seen and treated at a center with an experienced team of surgical oncologists, medical oncologists and radiation oncologists, specializing in the treatment of these tumors.
